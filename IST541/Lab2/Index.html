How to Make Bone Broth

By Zi Guo

Step 1: Select Your Bones

Choose a mix of:

  • marrow bones
  • knucklebones
  • meaty bones (beef, chicken, or pork)
    • The variety adds both flavor and nutrients.

    warning It is important to make sure the bones are fresh by checking theircoloration. Also mold can form when exposed to moisture and air, so store them in a cool, dry place away from sunlight if they are not being used immediately.

    Step 2: Clean the Bones by Boiling

    Pre-boil the bones briefly (you can add a cup of cooking alcohol if desired) to remove excess fat and impurities.

  • Skim and rinse before proceeding.

    • Step 3: Add Aromatics

    Place bones in a large pot or slow cooker.

  • Add onions, carrots, celery, garlic, and herbs (bay leaves, parsley, thyme).
  • A teaspoon of apple cider vinegar can help draw minerals from the bones (optional).

    • Step 4: Add Water
  • Add enough water to cover bones and vegetables by 2–3 inches.
  • Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce to a simmer. Skim foam as it rises.
  • For a touch of sweetness, add 2–3 dried dates or figs.

    • Step 5: Simmer Slowly
  • Simmer 12–24 hours for beef bones or 6–12 hours for chicken.
  • Top up with water as needed to keep bones submerged.
  • The long cook extracts collagen, calcium, and deep flavor.

    • Step 6: Final Adjustments & Storage

    Season to taste with salt. Cool slightly, strain, and store in jar up to 5 days in the refrigerator or up to 3 months in the freezer.
